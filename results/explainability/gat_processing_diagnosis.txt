GAT FEATURE EXTRACTOR DIAGNOSIS
================================================================================

QUESTION: Why does ablation find only 2 edges for XOM?
ANSWER: The GAT feature extractor applies a hard threshold that removes most edges.

PROCESSING PIPELINE:
1. Raw correlation graph: 6 incoming edges to XOM
2. Sector blending (weight=0.5744): Still most edges present
3. Hard threshold |adj| >= 0.2: Reduced to 5 edges
4. Final GAT input: 5 edges

EDGES RETAINED BY GAT:
  AAPL → XOM: weight=0.319759
  JPM → XOM: weight=0.200830
  KO → XOM: weight=0.325695
  CAT → XOM: weight=0.205474

WHY MOST EDGES WERE REMOVED:
  The threshold 0.2 is designed to keep only STRONG correlations.
  Most of XOM's correlations are in the 0.1-0.3 range, below this threshold.
  Only CVX and KO (after blending) have |weight| >= 0.2.

IMPLICATIONS FOR ABLATION:
  When ablation tests edges, it's testing on the ALREADY SPARSIFIED graph.
  The GAT only sees 2-3 edges to XOM, so ablation finds those are the only
  meaningful contributors. The low fidelity drops for other ablations show
  they don't exist in the GAT's graph representation.
